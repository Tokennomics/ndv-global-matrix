## 1. Executive Summary
The European Union’s current Cohesion Policy relies heavily on Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita as the primary metric for regional eligibility and funding allocation. This framework fails to account for ecological depletion, carbon health liabilities, and unpaid care dividends.

We propose **Cohesion Policy 2.0**, powered by the **Net Domestic Value (NDV)** engine. ## 2. Theoretical Foundation (NDV Master Equation)
The Net Domestic Value (NDV) replaces standard Gross Output (\(Y\)) with a comprehensive wealth-creation metric:

\[NDV = Y - D_p - D_n + \sum E^+ - \sum E^-\]

Where:
*   \(Y\): Gross Regional Product (GDP)
*   \(D_p\): Physical infrastructure depreciation
*   \(D_n\): Natural capital depletion (degraded land, water extraction, and emissions)
*   \(E^+\): Human and societal dividends (compounded education and the unpaid care shadow wage)
*   \(E^-\): Systemic debts and environmental frictions (e.g., smog health liabilities)

## 3. The Cohesion Policy 2.0 Algorithmic Transfer Mechanism
Under Cohesion 2.0, member states are classified into two archetypes based on their capital intensity and ecological role:
1.  **Industrial Hubs**: GDP per capita \(> \$45,000\) (high manufacturing/production concentration).
2.  **Natural Sinks**: GDP per capita \(\le \$45,000\) or region serving as a primary biosphere buffer.

### Mathematical Formulation
The transfer flow (\(T_i\)) for each member state \(i\) is computed as follows:

1.  **Industrial Hub Depletion Tax**:
    Each Industrial Hub \(j\) is assessed a tax proportional to its natural capital depreciation (\(D_{n,j}\)):
    \[T_j = -0.05 \times |D_{n,j}|\]
    This tax is deducted from the hub's final NDV and transferred to the Cohesion Pool.

2.  **Cohesion Pool Accumulation**:
    The total available funds in the Cohesion Pool (\(P_{cohesion}\)) represent the sum of all levies:
    \[P_{cohesion} = \sum_{j \in \text{Industrial}} 0.05 \times |D_{n,j}|\]

3.  **Biosphere Preservation Dividend (BPD)**:
    The Cohesion Pool is redistributed to Natural Sinks based on their relative share of ecological preservation capacity (measured by protected hectares, \(H_k\)):
    \[T_k = P_{cohesion} \times \left( \frac{H_k}{\sum_{m \in \text{Natural}} H_m} \right)\]
    Where \(k\) is a Natural Sink.

### Properties of the Transfer Matrix
*   **Budget Neutrality**: \(\sum_{i \in \text{EU}} T_i = 0\). The system is fully self-funding and requires no structural debt.
*   **Algorithmic Objectivity**: Transfers are calculated dynamically from physical and demographic data, eliminating political negotiation and lobbying.

## 4. Policy Implementation & Benefits

### A. Ending the "Broken Window Fallacy" in Cohesion Funding
Under the current GDP framework, regions that suffer environmental disasters receive rebuilding funds that boost GDP, masking net wealth destruction. NDV treats environmental disasters and health degradation as liabilities (\(E^-\) and \(D_n\)), penalizing the region's ledger and automatically triggering targeted cohesion payouts.

### B. Incentivizing Natural Capital Expansion
Because BPD payouts are tied directly to active ecological preservation area ratios, member states are financially incentivized to expand protected reserves, reforest agricultural margins, and clean urban airways to increase their share of the Cohesion Pool.

### C. Formalizing the Care Economy
Cohesion 2.0 integrates a shadow wage for domestic and care work (\(E^+_{care}\)), ensuring that countries with strong family support systems and community networks are credited with the wealth they generate, offsetting physical capital deficits.
